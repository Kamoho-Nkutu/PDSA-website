Signs Your Pet Needs Emergency Care

If your pet is showing any of these signs, please contact us immediately. When in doubt, it's always better to be safe and have your pet evaluated by our emergency team.

Difficulty Breathing

Rapid breathing, choking, gasping for air, or blue/grey gums

Trauma

Hit by car, falls from height, animal attacks, or other injuries

Poisoning

Ingestion of toxic foods, plants, medications, or chemicals

Seizures

Convulsions, tremors, or loss of consciousness

Bleeding

Uncontrolled bleeding from any part of the body

Collapse

Sudden inability to stand or loss of consciousness

Pain

Whining, trembling, reluctance to move, or aggression when touched

Distended Abdomen

Swollen, hard belly that may indicate bloat (GDV)

Pet First Aid Tips

While you should always seek veterinary care for emergencies, these first aid tips may help stabilize your pet before you can reach our clinic.

Emergency First Aid

Bleeding

Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth. Do not remove soaked cloths - add more layers.

Burns

Flush with cool water for 5-10 minutes. Do not apply ice or ointments.

Choking

If conscious, try to remove obstruction with pliers or tweezers. Be careful not to push further.

Heat Stroke

Move to cool area, apply cool (not cold) water to body, and offer small amounts of water.

Important First Aid Notes

First aid is not a substitute for veterinary care. Always contact us or your nearest emergency clinic after administering first aid. Never give human medications to pets unless specifically directed by a veterinarian. When transporting an injured pet, use a flat surface as a stretcher and muzzle animals in pain (unless vomiting or having breathing difficulties).
