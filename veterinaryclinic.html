Vaccinations are a crucial part of preventive healthcare for your pet. They help protect against serious illnesses and contribute to your pet's overall wellbeing.

Core Vaccines

Essential for all pets, protecting against widespread diseases like rabies, distemper, and parvovirus.

Non-Core Vaccines

Recommended based on your pet's specific lifestyle and risk factors, such as kennel cough or feline leukemia.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does my pet need vaccinations?

Puppies and kittens need a series of vaccinations every 3-4 weeks until they are 16 weeks old. Adult pets typically need booster shots annually or every three years, depending on the vaccine.

Are there any side effects to vaccinations?

Most pets experience no side effects. Some may have mild symptoms like lethargy or soreness at the injection site for a day or two. Serious reactions are rare but should be reported to your vet immediately.

Why are vaccines important?

Vaccines protect your pet from serious diseases, some of which can be fatal. They also help prevent the spread of diseases to other animals in the community.
